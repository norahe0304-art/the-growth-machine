# OpenAI Channel Memory

Distilled from The Growth Book research. Each channel below carries OpenAI's actual
precedent, drawn from documented cases, plus a fit rule for the machine's rollout
station. Use this file when a wave reaches SCALE and rollout has to pick where the
winning creative actually runs.

## TikTok: demo is the content

OpenAI's own TikTok account runs at roughly 840K followers and 5.1M likes by simply
posting product output as the feed itself: Sora preview clips dropped one after
another, with no separate ad layer on top (social-tiktok, ongoing). The account date
has no confirmed source, so rollout should never claim a founding date for it.

**What fits here:** raw generated output (video, motion, transformation) posted with
minimal captioning, so the demo carries the whole post. Anything with a strong before
and after beat, since that format has already proven out here.

**What does not fit:** static product screenshots, long-form explainer text, or any
asset that needs a caption to make sense. If the video does not work with the sound
off and no caption, it does not belong on this channel.

## X: the founder feed is the main stage

The heaviest official creative moments on X come from Sam Altman's personal account,
not the corporate one: the strawberry-photo tease (no-wall, 2024), the five-word
there is no wall reply, and the Ghibli profile-picture change all ran through his
feed. The official OpenAI account stays comparatively restrained and factual.

**What fits here:** short, low-production text or a single image, timed to a live
moment, ideally voiced as a specific person reacting rather than a brand announcing.
Text-only jokes and one-line rebuttals have outperformed produced creative here.

**What does not fit:** heavily produced video or campaign-style copy. X rewards
speed and specificity over polish on this channel, based on every case in the
research.

## Instagram: where official meme participation has precedent

ChatGPT's own Instagram account posted a pet-to-human transformation during that
trend's 2025 peak (pet-to-human, Apr 2025), the one confirmed case of an official
account joining a user meme with matching content rather than just observing it.

**What fits here:** a polished version of a live user trend, posted while the trend
is still climbing, using the same format users are already using. This is also the
one channel where the UGC design register (see design.md) is explicitly licensed,
because OpenAI itself has posted in that register here.

**What does not fit:** a trend response posted after the peak has passed. The
pet-to-human case worked because timing put OpenAI inside the wave while it climbed.

## YouTube and TV: brand films and Super Bowl scale

This is where OpenAI runs its most expensive, most narrative work: The Intelligence
Age (SB 2025) and You Can Just Build Things (SB 2026), plus the fall 2025 brand
campaign with Isle of Any and Miles Jay. These are slow-build, story-driven pieces
with production values the other channels never carry.

**What fits here:** a fully produced narrative concept with a beginning, middle, and
end, built around a real or realistic human story rather than a product feature list.

**What does not fit:** anything reactive or trend-timed. Nothing in the research
shows OpenAI using this channel for a fast-turnaround moment; everything here was
planned months ahead.

## In-app: where product features carry the joke

Several of OpenAI's biggest creative moments never left the product surface at all:
Santa Mode inside 12 Days of Shipmas (Shipmas, Dec 2024), the Monday voice persona
(Monday, Apr 2025), and the MS Paint doodle preset shipped directly into Images 2.0
after users demanded it by prompt (MS Paint, May 2026). This is the fastest channel
in the whole set: MS Paint went from a user prompt trend to a shipped preset inside
one product cycle.

**What fits here:** any winning insight that can become a literal feature, mode, or
preset rather than a piece of external creative. This is the channel rollout should
default to when a wave's SCALE verdict comes from a genuine product-shaped concept.

**What does not fit:** anything that requires external distribution to land. If the
concept only works as a post, it belongs on one of the other four channels instead.
