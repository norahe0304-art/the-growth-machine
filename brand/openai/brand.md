# OpenAI Brand Voice and Stance

Distilled from The Growth Book research: 45 documented cases of OpenAI creative and
growth behavior, 2022 through 2026. This file is the operator's read on how OpenAI
sounds and where it draws its lines. Every claim below cites a case for verification.

## Restraint over volume

OpenAI's owned brand work stays quiet. The first global brand campaign (fall 2025,
Isle of Any + Miles Jay) ran quiet slice-of-life footage. The two-wave OOH
run opened minimal (plain city boards, no logo shouting) before the second wave
turned the boards themselves into generated images (Reimagine anything, 2025). The
company lets user creativity supply the noise and keeps its own footprint calm.

## Accepting the meme, on the record

When the internet turns OpenAI or its people into a joke, the company's move is to
join, briefly and specifically, and let the joke stand. Sam Altman set his profile
picture to a Ghibli-style portrait during the March 2025 image wave (Ghibli, Mar 2025).
He replied to the strawberry-photo Strawberry guessing game with silence that fed the
theory, then killed a different rumor with five words: there is no wall (no-wall,
2024). ChatGPT's own Instagram account posted a pet-to-human transformation during
that trend's peak (pet-to-human, Apr 2025), the clearest instance of an official
account joining a user meme rather than just watching it. The pattern: one gesture,
timed at the peak, then OpenAI steps back out of the frame.

## Honesty about strain, framed as flex

OpenAI does not hide capacity problems. It states them, and lets the statement double
as proof of demand. Sam Altman's line during the Ghibli wave, our GPUs are melting,
turned an infrastructure incident into the headline (Ghibli, Mar 2025). The same wave
produced can yall please chill, a direct, informal address to users rather than a
throttling notice. The lesson for the machine: a real constraint, stated plainly, reads
as evidence of a moment that matters. A constraint dressed up as a marketing line reads
as a lie.

## The Intelligence Age throughline

Underneath the memes sits one serious narrative: this technology marks an epoch, and
OpenAI is building the infrastructure for it. The Super Bowl 2025 spot ran under that
exact title, The Intelligence Age (SB 2025). The Super Bowl 2026 spot, You Can Just
Build Things, kept the epoch claim implicit and let three small-business builders (a
seed farm, a scrapyard, a tamale shop) carry it instead (SB 2026). The throughline
never announces itself as a slogan in user-facing creative. It shows up as the reason
the small moments matter.

## Humor has a register: dry, a little tired, never a bit

OpenAI's official jokes read as deadpan and dry. Monday, the April Fools voice
persona, was built entirely around sardonic reluctance, described in its own materials
as Whatever (Monday, Apr 2025). The 1-800-CHATGPT launch demoed a rotary phone calling
an AI and played the incongruity completely straight (1-800, Dec 2024).
Twelve Days of Shipmas turned a release calendar into a running bit by repetition
alone, no jokes needed inside it (Shipmas, Dec 2024). None of these lean on slang,
memes-about-memes, or forced relatability.

## What OpenAI does not do

It does not perform youth. No official account has ever chased a dance trend or
youth-coded slang; the closest it gets is reposting what users already made (pet-to-
human, Ghibli pfp). It does not force its way into trends it did not seed and has no
real claim to. Action figures, Barbie boxes, and starter packs ran with zero official
participation (A3 to A5, CASES.md); OpenAI let three of the biggest 2025 waves pass
in total silence rather than bolt on a late, awkward comment. And it does not treat
copyright as optional. The Ghibli wave itself drew immediate copyright-concern
coverage even as OpenAI let it run (Ghibli, Mar 2025); Sora's opt-out-to-opt-in
reversal on cameo rights, forced by a three-day backlash, is the hard warning case
(Sora rights reversal, Oct 2025, see history.md). Any concept that requires a named
person, a named IP, or a named artist's style without a clear rights path fails this
brand's judgment before it reaches produce.

## How the machine uses this

When insight proposes a concept, it passes brand fit if it clears three checks:

1. **Restraint check.** Does the concept let the user's material carry the emotion,
   with OpenAI's presence limited to one clean gesture rather than a campaign push?
2. **Register check.** If the concept includes any voice, copy, or persona, is the
   humor dry and specific to the moment, never generic internet slang or a bit that
   requires OpenAI to perform an identity it has not earned?
3. **Rights check.** Does the concept use only the user's own material, or public,
   cleared assets? Any concept that leans on a named likeness, a named franchise, or
   an artist's identifiable style without a licensing path is killed here, before
   produce spends a single image call on it.
