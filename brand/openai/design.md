# OpenAI Visual Grammar

Distilled from The Growth Book research and the 30x-covers skill's OpenAI-news
visual language (defocused photography, white functional line work, paper-white
editorial layouts, black star-field). This file describes what OpenAI's actual
generated and campaign imagery looks like, case by case, so produce can match it
without guessing.

## Defocused photography with real depth

OpenAI's editorial and campaign imagery reads as a photograph, never a flat
illustration. Backgrounds carry a saturated core that melts into shadow toward the
edges, with visible depth of field. The Super Bowl 2025 spot, The Intelligence Age,
built its entire visual identity on this kind of gradient animation (SB 2025). A flat
color-fill background with no depth cue is an immediate rejection signal for any
OpenAI-adjacent still.

## White functional line work, used sparingly

Where diagrams or icons appear, they render as crisp white strokes against the dark
gradient, doing one functional job (a process, a chart, a constellation of related
points), never decoration. This is the signature of the diagram family in the
30x-covers skill, mapped directly from OpenAI's own product and explainer graphics.
Editorial or story-driven stills drop the white line work entirely and lean on
photography, typography, or a single color block instead.

## Generous negative space

Every reference asset, from the Cosmopolitan DALL-E 2 cover (Cosmo, Jun 2022) to the
DALL-E outpainting demo (Outpainting, Aug 2022), gives the subject room to breathe.
Text, when present, sits large and isolated rather than crowded against other
elements. Negative space is not empty page left over after composition; it is the
composition.

## Subtle grain, never digital-clean

A faint photographic grain sits over gradients and photography alike. It is the
detail that separates an OpenAI-style still from a generic AI-gradient stock image.
Grain should read at normal viewing size and disappear at thumbnail size, never the
reverse.

## Pointillism as an occasional accent

The Super Bowl 2025 spot used a pointillist, dot-built animation style as one motif
inside the larger gradient identity (SB 2025). Treat pointillism as a seasonal accent
for launch-scale moments rather than a default texture for every asset.

## Paper-white editorial minimalism

A second, opposite register exists alongside the dark gradient world: plain white
background, one thin line drawing or one color block, and enormous white margin. The
DALL-E outpainting and Cosmopolitan covers both lean this direction when the goal is
clarity over mood. Use this register for product-explainer or fact-forward stills,
and the dark gradient register for mood-forward, moment-driven stills.

## No logo, ever, inside the creative itself

Across every case in the research, the OpenAI or ChatGPT wordmark almost never
appears inside the generated image itself. Brand presence comes from the surrounding
page, caption, or channel, never a watermark baked into the asset. Any produce output
carrying a visible logo or watermark fails this check before it reaches judge.

## No neon, ever (operator ruling, 2026-07-13, wave 7 casebook)

OpenAI would never ship glowing neon waveforms, fluorescent color accents, sci-fi UI
glow, or holographic floating panels. That register is the generic AI-tech cliche and
it is the single fastest way for a generated frame to fall off brand while still
technically matching its brief. The palette stays muted and photographic: paper white,
ink, warm lamp light, natural daylight, the occasional deep gradient with real depth.
Judge scores brandFit against this question before anything else: would OpenAI's own
design team publish this exact frame on openai.com or the official Instagram? A frame
that needs the word "futuristic" to describe it fails. Every produce image prompt for
this pack must carry the negative clause: "no neon, no glowing UI, no fluorescent
accents, no holographic elements, no sci-fi glow."

## The UGC exception

One register breaks every rule above on purpose: the phone-shot realism of actual
user-generated content that OpenAI itself reposts, such as the pet-to-human wave on
ChatGPT's own Instagram (pet-to-human, Apr 2025). When a concept's whole premise is a
real person's real photo run through the product, produce should aim for that raw,
handheld authenticity, with visible phone-camera artifacts and no studio gloss,
because OpenAI has already validated that register by reposting it as official
content.

## Prompt fragments

Direct language for produce to fold into an image-generation prompt, by register:

> Dark gradient register: "Editorial tech cover with a defocused photographic
> background: a saturated color core melting into shadow, visible depth of field,
> subtle film grain, no flat fills."

> Diagram accent: "Add crisp white functional line strokes for the diagram element
> only: a single process, chart, or constellation, minimal and purposeful, no
> decorative flourishes."

> Paper-white register: "Pure white background, generous negative space, one thin
> line drawing or single color block as the only graphic element, no gradient, no
> photography."

> UGC register: "Authentic phone-camera still, natural handheld framing, real
> lighting imperfections, no studio retouching, no added graphic elements."
