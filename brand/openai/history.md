# OpenAI Track Record

The nine fires from The Growth Book's card data, each in three lines: the mechanic,
the scale numbers, and the official reaction. Then the formula the book distills
from them, the four laws that summarize the whole record, and the Sora warning. This
file is direct ammunition for the insight station: every named case here is a proven
asset-x-new-element pattern the machine can remix.

## The nine fires

**01. The Ghibli moment (Mar 2025).** Users ran their own photos through GPT-4o
image generation to get Studio Ghibli-style portraits.
Scale: 700M images in the first week, one million new users in one hour.
Reaction: CAUGHT. Sam Altman set his own profile picture to the style.

**02. The action-figure economy (Apr 2025).** Users boxed themselves as blister-pack
toys, job title as the figure name, three accessories as the bio.
Scale: 150M users in the first month of the image feature.
Reaction: MISSED. OpenAI never commented; only GPU throttling followed.

**03. Pets, but human (Apr 2025).** Owners asked what their dog or cat would look
like as a person and posted the uncanny results.
Scale: flooded TikTok, Reddit, Instagram, and Bluesky simultaneously.
Reaction: CAUGHT. ChatGPT's own Instagram account posted a pet-to-human transformation.

**04. Twelve days of shipping (Dec 2024).** OpenAI serialized its own launch
calendar into twelve weekday drops across December, including Sora, o1, and Santa Mode.
Scale: twelve consecutive launches, live-blogged for two straight weeks by the tech press.
Reaction: CAUGHT (official-originated). This case is OpenAI's own creative act, an
in-house serialization stunt rather than a user wave it joined.

**05. Wrapped, the sequel (Dec 2025).** Users invented DIY Spotify-style year-end
recaps in Dec 2024; a year later OpenAI shipped an official version with an em-dash counter.
Scale: feeds filled with shared recap cards within 24 hours of launch.
Reaction: CAUGHT. OpenAI productized a full year after the user version first appeared.

**06. Monday, a personality experiment (Apr 2025).** OpenAI shipped a sardonic,
reluctant voice persona on April Fools' Day, available to every tier.
Scale: shipped free to all tiers on day one.
Reaction: CAUGHT (official-originated). Users petitioned for its return after it was
retired, the clearest signal a bit landed.

**07. Sam Altman, the meme (2024 to 2025).** A garden photo teased the o1 model
under the Strawberry codename; five words, there is no wall, answered scaling
skeptics; his Ghibli profile picture crowned the image wave.
Scale: zero paid budget behind any of the three moments.
Reaction: CAUGHT. Global press coverage followed each one without a press release
driving it.

**08. Dial 1-800-CHATGPT (Dec 2024).** OpenAI gave ChatGPT a toll-free number and a
WhatsApp line, demoed live on a rotary phone.
Scale: 15 to 30 minutes of free monthly access with no account required.
Reaction: CAUGHT (official-originated). Coverage called it a direct jab at Meta AI's
distribution.

**09. The ugly-drawing preset (May 2026).** Users demanded deliberately clumsy,
scribbly, pathetic-looking images as a reaction against AI polish.
Scale: covered by Forbes and TechRadar within the same week the trend peaked.
Reaction: CAUGHT, fastest turn on record. Sam Altman reshared the prompt and Images
2.0 shipped it as a built-in preset inside one product cycle.

## The formula

Existing asset x one new element = identity content people share. Every fire above
ran on material the user already owned; the product supplied exactly one new
element on top of it, and the user supplied the emotion that made it worth posting.

## The four laws

**Law 1.** People share what makes them look interesting. Every fire put the user in
the subject line: their face, their pet, their year, their taste.

**Law 2.** Attention needs four parts familiar and one part strange. The fires that
traveled furthest were known formats wearing exactly one new element, like a wedding
photo redrawn in Miyazaki light.

**Law 3.** Growth arrives at peak need and stays through stored history. Retention
comes from the chats, memory, and habits a product keeps for its user, which is the
exact asset Sora never built.

**Law 4.** Speed decides who owns the moment. The window lasts days at most. The
Ghibli wave was caught within hours; the 150M-user action-figure wave passed in
total official silence.

## The Sora warning

Sora proved every ignition mechanic in this file can still die: the cameo feature
hit 1M downloads in five days and topped the App Store within a week (cameo launch,
Sep to Oct 2025), yet 7-day retention fell to 2 percent and 30-day retention to 1
percent, burning roughly $15M a day against $2.1M in cumulative revenue before
OpenAI shut it down (Feb to Apr 2026, see CASES.md Part D). The app also forced a
three-day emergency reversal of its cameo rights model, from opt-out to opt-in with
revenue share, after a copyright backlash, the single clearest case in the whole
research set for the rights check in brand.md. Ignition without a retention asset,
and creative freedom without a rights gate, both end the same way.
